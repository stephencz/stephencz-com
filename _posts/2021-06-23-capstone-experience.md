At Rowan University, the last class in my coursework as a Computing and Informatics major was the Capstone Experience.
The class consisted of a group project with the goal of developing a website or application that uses data from at least three different APIs.
My group consisted of four members, including myself, and, as the most experienced, I took the lead.
For our project, we decided to make a website to display information about the top fifty highest rated video games of all time.

You can find the [full source code on GitHub](https://github.com/stephencz/rowan-ci-capstone).

## The Group

Out of the four members in my group, I was the most experienced programmer. 
Computing and Informatics is an interesting major because it is designed to allow a high degree of customization.
Unlike Computer Science, not all Computing and Informatics majors want to become programmers.
Many have an interest in cybersecurity or more general information technology fields.

As the most experienced, I took it upon myself to lead the way for the others.
I made an effort to get my work done as soon as possible, so I could offer insight into that phase of the project.
I created a Discord server to give my team members direct access to me, so that I could assist them. 
And, at the start of each phase of the project I recorded a YouTube video detailing my approach to that phase.

## The APIs
For our project, we decided to build a website that would display information about the top fifty highest rated video games of all time.
To do this we decided to use the following four APIs:

* [IGDB API](https://www.igdb.com)
* [Youtube Data API](https://developers.google.com/youtube/v3)
* [Bing Image Search API](https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/bing/apis/bing-image-search-api)
* [Reddit API](https://www.reddit.com/dev/api/)
