*Cream of Potato* is a programming blog which focuses on publishing complete, scholarly-like, articles.

I've found that the Internet is becoming an increasingly useless tool.
The daily upload of short, unhelpful, incomplete, low-quality content is more or less the slowest DDOS attack ever performed.
The fantastic content, which I know is out there, gets lost in a sea of... well... shit.

This website is an attempt at producing fantastic content.
My goal is to produce articles which are useful to the reader.
I want articles to be complete in the sense that they answer more questions than they create.
And high-quality in the sense that they produce a new understanding within the mind of the reader.

Naturally, most of the articles on this website are written to inform. 

I try to write in a way that teaches.
Effective teaching requires a delicate balance and meshing of techniques.
People learn in different ways. 
I am what I've heard called an "intuitive learner" and a "kinesthetic learner".
I learn by grasping the big picture ideas and by applying those ideas to useful, real-world examples.

You might be a visual learner.
Or you might require analogies.
Or maybe you learn best with pure facts.

In any case, I try to structure my writing in a way that introduces the big picture idea first, and then expands on it with a variety of tools such as pictures, analogies, and exercises.

Finally, I call the articles on this website scholarly-like, because that is what they are.
As I have said, I want my content to be useful. 
Scholarly texts have a tendency to get bogged down in their rigour.
While exhaustive details may be useful to the select individual, gaining new understanding often requires a bird's eye view.
I leave the refinement and exploration of the nitty-gritty to someone else.

### Organization
This website's content is divided into three types: Pages, articles, and guides.

This is a page.
It is a dateless, categoryless, blob of information related to the website itself.

Articles are where the content live.
Every article has a date, and can belong to multiple categories.
Each category has [a dedicated page](/categories) which lists all articles belonging to it. 
Articles show up on the home page feed as they are published.
And a chronological list of every article can be found on the [Archive](/archive) page.

Articles can, but do not have to, belong to guides.
Guides transcends categories in that they have an extra utility.
The purpose of a guide is to group content that will promote the understanding of some larger topic.
Where a category is a mere listing of related articles, a guide is a structured listing of articles with additional information.

A list of guides can be found on the [Guides](/guides) page.
